Frequently Asked Questions

What is a TNBFaucet Testnet?

Testnet is an alternative thenewboston network. TNBFaucet coins are separate and distinct from actual TNB coins and are never supposed to have any value.

It is used for testing decentralised apps and smart contracts without spending real money.

It has the same properties as usual TNB coins:
  • You can use same account address
  • You can transfer and receive it
  • Test network behaves the same as mainnet
The main difference is that it has 0 value.
  • You cannot buy/sell it on any exchange
  • Everyone can just get some for free
  • Test network is much smaller in size
How do I start developing on the TNBFaucet testnet?
You would need to:
  • Connect to testnet by switching your active bank to one of the testnet bank nodes.

  • Request some free testnet coins to your address by creating a social media post.
  • Use testnet coins in your applications for testing/ development purposes
Why do I have to post on social media to collect free coins?

To prevent a single account from accumulating all the coins, requests are made using a public facebook or twitter account.

Social media posts also help spreading awareness about thenewboston and it is a great way for people to learn how everything works without losing real coins.

Will I receive coins every “x” hours?

No, to receive more coins you would have to create new social media post. Every “x” hour is a limit to prevent accumulating all the coins by a single person. TNBFaucet provides a few options to receive testnet coins in different amount based upon your requirements, each having different limit in hours.

What is the difference between a mainnet and a testnet?

The “mainnet” is what you are familiar with as a user - the instance of the blockchain where the cryptocurrency has value.

While the “testnet” is essentially a developer playground - it is where new versions of the software get tested, and where people can write and test their apps without risking losing real money

Where can I find my account number? Is there any wallet for TNBFaucet?

Any thenewboston wallet including official Account Manager desktop application can be used to connect to TNBFaucet as long as your active bank is one of the testnet bank nodes

You can create new account the same way like you did for mainnet or use the existing one to receive free TNBFaucet coins.

Why don't people run apps on the testnet? I don't care about the "value" and it's free!?

Without economic incentives, no banks/ validators would join testnet and the entirety of the blockchain security model fails. Therefore, the testnet is good to well, ‘test’ your applications at almost no cost, but is a terrible location to have your apps in production. This is why size and scale of testnet is much smaller. Even the testnet coins do not hold any value, anyone with a social media account can collect them for free.
